# Vertical Analysis Section - Layman's Guide

## What is This Section?

Vertical Analysis (also called Common-Size Analysis) shows each financial item as a percentage of a base figure. Instead of comparing absolute numbers across years, it shows the "composition" or "structure" of financial statements. Think of it like a pie chart - it shows what percentage each piece represents of the whole.

## What Information Does It Show?

### Income Statement Vertical Analysis
Each item shown as **% of Net Revenues**:
- Net Revenues: 100% (the base)
- Cost of Goods Sold: X% of revenue
- Gross Margin: X% of revenue
- Operating Expenses: X% of revenue
- EBIT: X% of revenue
- Net Income: X% of revenue

### Balance Sheet Vertical Analysis
Each item shown as **% of Total Assets**:
- Total Assets: 100% (the base)
- Total Equity: X% of assets
- Total Debt: X% of assets

## Why Is This Important?

Vertical Analysis helps you:
- **See Structure**: What percentage of revenue goes to costs vs. profit?
- **Compare Efficiency**: Is the company becoming more efficient over time?
- **Identify Changes**: Has the cost structure changed?
- **Benchmark**: Compare to industry standards
- **Understand Margins**: See profitability as percentages

## Common Questions You Might Be Asked

1. **"What is Vertical Analysis?"**
   - It shows each financial item as a percentage of a base figure. For income statements, everything is % of revenue. For balance sheets, everything is % of total assets.

2. **"Why use percentages instead of actual numbers?"**
   - Percentages make it easy to compare the "structure" or "composition" regardless of company size. A 20% profit margin means the same thing whether revenue is â‚¬1B or â‚¬10B.

3. **"What does 'COGS is 19.7% of revenue' mean?"**
   - It means for every â‚¬100 PRADA makes in sales, â‚¬19.70 goes to the cost of making/buying the products. The lower this percentage, the better (more profit margin).

4. **"What does 'EBIT is 22.5% of revenue' mean?"**
   - It means for every â‚¬100 in sales, PRADA makes â‚¬22.50 in operating profit. This is the EBIT margin - a key profitability metric.

5. **"Why is Net Revenues always 100%?"**
   - Because it's the base figure. Everything else is compared to it. If revenue is the "whole pie," other items show what percentage of that pie they represent.

6. **"What does an 'increasing' trend mean?"**
   - It depends on the item! For profit items (EBIT, Net Income), increasing is good. For cost items (COGS, Expenses), decreasing is good.

7. **"How do I know if a change is good or bad?"**
   - **Good**: Profit percentages increasing, cost percentages decreasing
   - **Bad**: Profit percentages decreasing, cost percentages increasing
   - PRADA shows all good trends!

8. **"What's the difference between Horizontal and Vertical Analysis?"**
   - **Horizontal**: Compares the same item across years (growth over time)
   - **Vertical**: Shows each item as % of base (structure/composition)
