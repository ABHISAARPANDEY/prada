# Trends Section - Layman's Guide

## What is This Section?

The Trends section shows year-over-year growth rates - how fast PRADA is growing. It's like tracking your weight loss or salary increases - it shows the rate of change, not just the absolute numbers. This section answers: "How fast is PRADA growing?"

## What Information Does It Show?

### Growth Rate Chart
A line chart showing:
- **Revenue Growth %**: How fast sales are growing year-over-year
- **Net Income Growth %**: How fast profit is growing year-over-year

### Key Insights
Two explanation cards:
1. **Revenue Growth**: Analysis of sales growth trends
2. **Margin Expansion**: Analysis of profitability improvement

## Why Is This Important?

Trends help you:
- **See Momentum**: Is growth accelerating or slowing?
- **Compare Growth Rates**: Which is growing faster - revenue or profit?
- **Predict Future**: Consistent growth suggests future success
- **Identify Patterns**: Are there seasonal or cyclical patterns?
- **Assess Sustainability**: Is growth sustainable or a one-time spike?

## Common Questions You Might Be Asked

1. **"What is Year-over-Year Growth?"**
   - It's the percentage change from one year to the next. If revenue was €4.20B in 2022 and €4.73B in 2023, that's 12.5% growth.

2. **"What does the growth chart show?"**
   - It shows how fast PRADA is growing each year. The lines show growth rates - higher lines = faster growth.

3. **"Why is 2021 growth 0%?"**
   - Because 2021 is the first year in our analysis, there's no previous year to compare to. Growth starts from 2022.

4. **"What does 24.8% revenue growth mean?"**
   - It means PRADA's sales grew by 24.8% from 2021 to 2022. That's very strong growth - almost 25% more sales!

5. **"Why is Net Income growth higher than Revenue growth?"**
   - Excellent question! This means PRADA is becoming more efficient - they're not just selling more, they're making more profit from each sale. This is a very good sign!

6. **"What is Margin Expansion?"**
   - It means profit margins are increasing. PRADA is keeping a larger percentage of each sale as profit. This happens when they can charge higher prices or reduce costs.

7. **"Is 12.5% growth good?"**
   - Yes! For a mature luxury brand, 12.5% revenue growth is excellent. Most companies would be happy with 5-10% growth.

8. **"What does the trend tell us about the future?"**
   - Consistent positive growth suggests PRADA is on a good trajectory. However, past performance doesn't guarantee future results, but it's a positive indicator.

9. **"Why do growth rates vary?"**
   - Many factors: market conditions, new product launches, store openings, economic factors, competition, etc. PRADA's growth has been strong but varied.

- **Margin Expansion**: How efficiently your car uses fuel (efficiency)

If you're driving fast AND accelerating AND using fuel efficiently, you're doing great! That's PRADA's situation.

## Important Concepts Explained Simply

### Revenue Growth Rate
- **Formula**: (Current Year - Previous Year) / Previous Year × 100
- **What it means**: How fast sales are increasing
- **PRADA's**: 24.8% (2022), 12.5% (2023) = strong growth
- **Why it matters**: Growing revenue = expanding business

### Net Income Growth Rate
- **Formula**: (Current Year - Previous Year) / Previous Year × 100
- **What it means**: How fast profit is increasing
- **PRADA's**: 58% (2022), 44% (2023) = exceptional growth
- **Why it matters**: Profit growing faster than revenue = becoming more efficient

### Margin Expansion
- **What it is**: Profit margins increasing over time
- **PRADA's**: EBIT margin 14.5% → 22.5%, Net margin 8.8% → 14.2%
- **Why it matters**: Keeping more of each sale as profit
- **How it happens**: Higher prices, lower costs, or both
