# Ratios Section - Layman's Guide

## What is This Section?

The Ratios section shows financial ratios - these are calculated numbers that help you understand PRADA's financial health, profitability, and efficiency. Think of ratios like a "financial health scorecard" - they give you quick insights into how well the company is performing.

## What Information Does It Show?

### Profitability Ratios
1. **Gross Margin %**: Profit from sales as % of revenue
2. **EBIT Margin %**: Operating profit as % of revenue
3. **Net Margin %**: Final profit as % of revenue

### Leverage Ratios
4. **Debt-to-Equity %**: How much debt vs. equity (financial risk)
5. **Return on Equity (ROE) %**: How well they use owners' money to generate profit

### Charts
- **Profitability Chart**: Shows all three margin ratios over 3 years
- **Leverage Chart**: Shows debt-to-equity and ROE over 3 years

## Common Questions You Might Be Asked

1. **"What is a Financial Ratio?"**
   - A ratio is a calculated number that compares two financial items. It helps you understand relationships and performance. Like "miles per gallon" for a car - it's a useful comparison metric.

2. **"What is Gross Margin %?"**
   - It's the percentage of revenue that becomes gross profit (after subtracting cost of goods sold). PRADA's 80.4% means for every â‚¬100 in sales, they make â‚¬80.40 in gross profit.

3. **"What is EBIT Margin %?"**
   - It's operating profit as a percentage of revenue. PRADA's 22.5% means for every â‚¬100 in sales, they make â‚¬22.50 in operating profit (before interest and taxes).

4. **"What is Net Margin %?"**
   - It's final profit as a percentage of revenue. PRADA's 14.2% means for every â‚¬100 in sales, they keep â‚¬14.20 as final profit (after all expenses and taxes).

5. **"What is Debt-to-Equity Ratio?"**
   - It shows how much debt PRADA has compared to equity. A lower ratio is generally better (less risk). PRADA's ratio is healthy, meaning they're not over-leveraged.

6. **"What is Return on Equity (ROE)?"**
   - It shows how well PRADA uses shareholders' money to generate profit. Higher ROE = better use of capital. PRADA's ROE improved, showing they're using money more efficiently.

7. **"Why are margins important?"**
   - Margins show profitability efficiency. Higher margins = more profit from each sale = better business performance. PRADA's improving margins show they're becoming more profitable.

8. **"What's a good margin?"**
   - It depends on the industry! For luxury fashion, PRADA's margins are excellent:
   - Gross Margin: 80%+ (excellent - luxury brands can charge premium prices)
   - EBIT Margin: 22.5% (very good)
   - Net Margin: 14.2% (strong)

9. **"How do I read the ratio charts?"**
   - Look at the trend lines:
   - **Going up** = improving (good for profit margins, ROE)
   - **Going down** = declining (good for debt-to-equity if decreasing)
   - **All PRADA's profit ratios going up** = excellent performance!
