## Common Questions You Might Be Asked

1. **"What is Cash Flow?"**
   - It's the actual money moving in and out of the company. Unlike profit (which can include non-cash items), cash flow shows real money.

2. **"Why is Operating Cash Flow positive?"**
   - Good sign! It means PRADA is making real money from selling products. This is the most important cash flow - it's the core business.

3. **"Why is Investing Cash Flow negative?"**
   - That's normal! It means PRADA is spending money on long-term assets: opening stores, buying equipment, making investments. This is investing in future growth.

4. **"What does negative Financing Cash Flow mean?"**
   - It means PRADA is paying back loans, paying dividends, or buying back shares. This shows financial discipline.

5. **"What's a healthy cash flow pattern?"**
   - **Operating**: Strongly positive (making money) ✅ PRADA has this
   - **Investing**: Negative (investing in growth) ✅ PRADA has this
   - **Financing**: Can vary (depends on strategy) ✅ PRADA manages this

6. **"How do I read the bars?"**
   - **Bars above zero**: Money coming in
   - **Bars below zero**: Money going out
   - **Taller bars**: More money (in or out)

7. **"What if Operating Cash Flow is negative?"**
   - That would be very concerning - it means the company isn't making money from operations. But PRADA's is strongly positive ✅

8. **"Is negative Investing Cash Flow bad?"**
   - No! It's normal and often good - it means the company is investing in growth. PRADA is expanding, which requires investment.

9. **"What does the chart tell us about PRADA's financial health?"**
   - **Strong operating cash flow**: Making money ✅
   - **Investing in growth**: Spending on expansion ✅
   - **Managing debt**: Paying back loans ✅
   - **Overall**: Healthy cash flow pattern ✅

For healthy personal finance:

- Big purchases (investing) are normal but reduce cash
