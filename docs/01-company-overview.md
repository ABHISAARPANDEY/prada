# Company Overview Section - Layman's Guide

## What is This Section?

The Company Overview section is like an introduction to PRADA. It tells you the basics about the company - who they are, what they do, where they operate, and how big they are. Think of it as the "About Us" page of a company website, but focused on business facts.

## What Information Does It Show?

### Company Facts
- **Founded**: When the company started (1913)
- **Headquarters**: Where the main office is (Milan, Italy)
- **Stock Exchange**: Where you can buy their shares (Hong Kong Stock Exchange)
- **Employees**: How many people work for PRADA
- **Stores**: How many retail locations they have worldwide

### Company Description
- What PRADA does (luxury fashion, handbags, clothing, accessories)
- Their reputation in the market
- Their position in the luxury industry

## Why Is This Important?

Before diving into financial numbers, you need to understand:
- **What business they're in**: This helps you understand their revenue sources
- **How big they are**: Employee and store count shows scale
- **Where they operate**: Global presence affects financial performance
- **Their market position**: Being a luxury brand explains their pricing and margins

## Common Questions You Might Be Asked

1. **"What does PRADA do?"**
   - PRADA is a luxury fashion company that makes high-end handbags, clothing, shoes, and accessories. They're known for premium quality and Italian craftsmanship.

2. **"How old is PRADA?"**
   - PRADA was founded in 1913, making it over 110 years old. It started as a small leather goods shop in Milan.

3. **"Where is PRADA based?"**
   - PRADA's headquarters is in Milan, Italy, but they have stores and operations worldwide.

4. **"Is PRADA a big company?"**
   - Yes, PRADA is a major global luxury brand with hundreds of stores worldwide and thousands of employees.

5. **"Can I buy PRADA stock?"**
   - Yes, PRADA Group is publicly traded on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (stock code: 1913).

## Real-World Analogy

Think of the Company Overview like a company's business card or resume. Just like you'd want to know someone's name, job title, and where they work before learning about their salary, you want to know PRADA's basic facts before understanding their financial performance.

- They're a publicly traded company
